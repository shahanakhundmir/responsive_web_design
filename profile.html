My Journey back to Tech

Hey, I'm Shahana someone who used to code. Still loves to code. And ... hopes to code a bit more.

An Introduction to Me

SHORT SUMMARY

I started coding when I was around 11, using a BBC Basic computer hooked up to an old black & white.
We loaded our games from tapes and printed on a dot matrix that screeched everytime it moved to the next line.
We couldn't afford that computer, by chance we inherited it when a close friend emigrated and left it behind.
I learnt to code by copying from the back of the computer's user guide.
When I was young not many girls coded, but I was pretty determined to do what I loved.
So I believe that my journey was a lot of luck,
a lot of being in the right place at the right time,
but ...... that's how you get to the place that your meant to be.

MY EXPERIENCE

I began my career as a Java programmer, working in application support.
Leaving this when I had my kids, I spent the next five to six years working as a project manager in community projects.
Around five years ago, I set up The Cookie Box which was my other passion of baking,
But 18 months ago I decided to give a career in Tech another go. Recently I worked for the charity sector on an analysis of funding to the BAME community after Covid-19.

Organised.

If you feel low, I have a baking recipe that can fix it.

Gettting back to Tech

It was August 2019 and I was at a cross-roads with my company & at the same time, my daughter Jannah was embarking on her own journey into Tech.
She dragged me to a Women in Tech event at IBM in Novemeber and we were the last to leave.
Inspired by the strong and talented women that I heard speaking, I decided to try coding again.
It began with a YouTube Python course, but I was quickly hooked.
Within a few months I had closed my business and set out to learn Python with a view to returning to Tech.

Coding was something that I had always loved but convinced myself that I could not go back to.
Taking the first step is always the hardest, but if you do something you enjoy, the journey becomes easy.
